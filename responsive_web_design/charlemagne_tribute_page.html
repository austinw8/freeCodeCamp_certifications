The "Father of Europe"

Charlemagne's Legacy

  • United Western Europe - Expanded the Frankish Kingdom to cover modern-day France, Germany, and Italy, creating the largest empire in Europe since Rome.
  • Crowned Emperor - Revived the Roman Empire in the West as the Holy Roman Emperor, strengthening ties with the Catholic Church.
  • Conquered the Lombards - Became King of the Lombards, defending the Papal States and solidifying his authority in Italy.
  • Christianized Europe - Forced the Saxons and other conquered peoples to convert to Christianity, spreading the faith across Europe.
  • Carolingian Renaissance - Revitalized education, preserved classical texts, and promoted Latin literacy through schools and reforms.
  • Created Carolingian Minuscule - Introduced a standardized, legible writing system that influenced modern European scripts.
  • Legal Reforms - Issued capitulary laws to organize governance, justice, and administration, laying the foundation for feudalism.
  • Missi Dominici - Appointed royal envoys to enforce laws and oversee local rulers, ensuring effective administration.
  • Defeated the Avars - Destroyed the Avar Khanate, extending Frankish rule into Central Europe and securing trade routes.
  • Build the Aachen Capital - Established Aachen as his royal court and cultural center, symbolizing imperial power and intellectual growth.
